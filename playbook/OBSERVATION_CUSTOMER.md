# Customer Observation Guide

The merchants' own audiences are DOF's first customers — by design (the share
journey, Release 1.1). Observation here is mostly passive: real links, real
group chats, real strangers.

## How merchants recruit their customers

- Day 3–4 of the fortnight: each merchant shares **their shop link** (not a
  product — the shop; the unfurl leads with their story) to wherever their
  audience already lives: IG story, WhatsApp group, market newsletter.
- The observer does nothing except confirm the unfurl rendered correctly
  *before* it goes out. A broken preview in front of their audience is an S1.
- One merchant per cohort also shares a **deal link** and one a **spark link**
  — three arrival surfaces, observed once each.

## What to observe (passively, via existing data + merchant reports)

| Question | Where the answer lives |
|---|---|
| Did anyone arrive? | merchant asks their chat; fires/follows appearing (`store_follows`, reactions) |
| Did arrivals engage? | fire/follow/save counts moving within 24h of the share |
| Did anyone come back? | engagement rows on later calendar days (the ledger's active-day proxy) |
| Did anyone keep a corner? | `identity_claims` (claim_type='visitor') |
| Did the loop continue? | a customer following a *second* merchant (cross-store follows) |
| Did demand appear? | **any customer asking a merchant "how do I buy?"** — report verbatim |

That last row matters most: it is the only honest signal for when commerce
infrastructure (orders) earns its place on the roadmap.

## Live observation (optional, two customers max)

If a merchant can invite one friendly customer to a 10-minute screen-share:
watch them open the shared link cold. Watch-points: do they scroll the shelf or
read the story; do they understand Fire without explanation; do they find
"More shops on DOF"; does the Keep-your-corner card read as an offer or a
signup wall (their words, verbatim).

## What constitutes success (per merchant, first week)

1. ≥1 **stranger** (not the merchant, not the observer) follows the store
   without being coached.
2. ≥1 visitor returns on a later day (engagement rows on ≥2 distinct days).
3. The merchant can name one thing a customer said to them about the shop.

Zero across all three after a genuine share is itself a finding — it means the
share surfaces or the arrival experience are the bottleneck, and the friction
log should say which.
