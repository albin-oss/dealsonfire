# Merchant Observation Guide

One merchant, one observer, one first hour. Sit beside them (or screen-share).
Phone or laptop — **their** device, **their** photos, **their** words.

## Protocol

- Say once, at the start: *"I'm going to be quiet and watch. Talk out loud if
  you like. If you get stuck, try whatever you'd try if I weren't here."*
- **Do not interrupt.** Not to help, not to explain, not to defend the product.
- If they ask a direct question, answer with: *"What would you try?"* Answer
  for real only if they are fully blocked — then log it as S2 minimum (a
  blocked journey needed a human).
- Timestamp every note. Silence longer than ~10 seconds on one screen is a
  **hesitation** — log where their eyes are.
- Log **verbatim words**, not paraphrases. "What's a spark?" is data; "was
  confused by sparks" is interpretation.

## Signals to record (every journey)

| Signal | What it looks like |
|---|---|
| Confusion | re-reading, backtracking, wrong tap, "hm?" |
| Hesitation | >10s stillness, hovering, switching tabs |
| Question | anything they say aloud with a question mark |
| Unexpected behavior | any path the product didn't intend |
| Delight | unprompted smile, "oh nice", showing someone, screenshotting |

## The journeys, in order, with known watch-points

### 0 · Arrival & account (session mode — first real test ever)
Register → verify email → land. **Watch-points:** does the verification email
arrive and read clearly on their phone; do they stall at the password; do they
end up signed in without help. This path has never met a real user.

### 1 · Ignite (store creation, ~4 min promise)
**Watch-points:** the handle moment (do they get their name?); do they read
the drafts or accept blindly; the launch moment — do they *react* when the
store goes live; do they immediately try to see it.

### 2 · Identity (/store — tagline, story, promise)
**Watch-points:** do they write in their own voice or accept the draft
verbatim; does "promise" make sense as a concept; do they check the live
storefront afterward without being told it's possible.

### 3 · Product (/products — the one-line composer)
**Watch-points:** do they type a real one-liner or hunt for a form; **price
entry** (the field commits on blur — watch for "did it take?"); photo upload
from a real camera roll (HEIC, size, patience); do they understand
"● On your store / ○ Not on your store" without asking.

### 4 · Deal (/deals)
**Watch-points:** do they grasp that a deal points at an on-store product; is
the headline theirs or the placeholder's echo; do they use View live / Copy
link unprompted.

### 5 · Spark (/sparks)
**Watch-points:** the word "spark" itself — do they know what to write without
an example; do they attach a product; **the moment after publishing** — where
do they go next? (The product bets: View live.)

### 6 · Share (storefront → Share this shop)
**Watch-points:** which channel they reach for (WhatsApp? IG story?); do they
check how the unfurl looks before sending; their words when they caption it —
*that caption is DOF's real positioning, in merchant language. Write it down.*
